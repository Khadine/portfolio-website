Trail Goods:

A Plant Adventure Hiking App

UI Design Case Study- Native Mobile (iOS + Android) app

Introducing:
Trail Goods

A chance for hikers and nature enthusiasts to experience and learn about the natural world around us.

Trail Goods is a mobile application that can be downloaded on both iOS and Android devices for use during hiking or walking on trails.

This case study provides a comprehensive overview of the UX and UI design process, including the highlights and challenges faced during the project. Additionally, a selection of the deliverables will be presented.

Project Timeline

Project Description

Our Project brief was to design the iOS and Android version of a plant adventure hiking mobile app.

While creating the apps design and layout, I made sure to follow both the iOS and Material Design Guidelines for each version of the app.

It was crucial to ensure that the UI patterns and layouts for each app were user-friendly and consistent within their respective platforms.

Market Research

Before beginning the design, I researched popular apps in the competitive market. My focus was on Seek by iNaturalist, which helps identify flora and fauna, Natural Atlas, a hiking app with limited plant-identifying features, and All Trails, a well-known hiking app.

Through my research, I gained valuable insight into the user base of each app and what they offered. I also identified areas where each app could improve and what features were necessary for an app to function as a user would like.

What's my Objective?

After researching competitors in the market, I noticed that most existing apps separated the tracking of hikes and identification of plants and Fungi. This meant that users had to download multiple apps for their needs, which can be inconvenient while on a hike.

Trail Goods solves this problem by providing users with a one-stop-shop solution. It allows users to research hikes and track their activity stats, walking time, elevation gain, and distance while also enabling them to identify Flora and Fungi using their device’s camera or by searching for species name.

With Trail Goods, users can enjoy all the benefits of a regular hiking app with the added benefit of identify plants and Fungi, without having to switch between multiple apps.

User Persona

User personas are a great way to keep an apps design user centric.

Throughout the apps creation, I frequently referred to this persona to ensure that the app remained focused on user needs and expectations.

User Flow

As both the UI Designer and a user of hiking and plant/fungi identifier apps, I took the time to understand what functions a user would need to perform with in the app. With this in mind, I prioritized user flows that were crucial to my design process.

These flows include:

  • Allow users to search for plants and fungi
  • Be able to search for and start a new trail or hiking activity
  • View all plants, fungi and trail heads that were saved/logged on a map

Challenges

During this project, I faced challenges due to my limited knowledge of Android's platform system. Despite its widespread popularity as a mobile device, I personally have never worked with it.

However, I was fortunate enough to have access to an Android device during the design phase. This enabled me to gain valuable experience with Android's unique UI patterns and design options that differ from those commonly found on iOS.

A Major Change

Initially, my app's navigation was simple with a home page, search page, and map page in that order.

However, after looking at my competitors' apps, I realized the positive impact of presenting the map page first.

As a user, seeing my pins and hard work on the map made me feel accomplished and energized.

This sentiment was confirmed during user testing.

Final Screens

iOS

Login Screen

Users begin their experience by logging in or signing up.

Goods Map

Here users will be able to add location pins for plants, fungi and trail heads, as well as log activities.

Nature Tab

The Nature Tab will show users their local ecology based on their location. They will also be able to view the weather forecast, making planning a hiking activity easier and localized from within the app.

Search Tab

The Search Tab gives users the tools to properly search for Plants, Fungi or Trailheads. Users can search by category, filters or in-app camera feature.

The Fungi of the day information section is updated daily with new plants, fungi and hiking suggestions, to regularly engage users.

Camera Feature

Within the app, users can use their mobile device's camera to identify plants or fungi. Once they take a photo, the app will direct them to the plant’s information page, provide a list of possible matches, or offer suggestions if no match is found.

Filter Options

Using the Filter Feature, users will be able to choose from specific options within each category. This will allow the app to provide the user with a list of possible results.

The Search for a Hiking Route

With the text input fields, users can conveniently search for hiking routes. The hiking routes information page allows users to view a trail preview, trail stats, and pin the trail to their Goods Map. The hikes info page will also provide users with a description of the hike, including a glimpse of the flora and fungi found along the trail.

Additionally, while out on a hike users will be able to access the in-app camera feature, allowing them to identify and log plants and fungi.
